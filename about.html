ABOUT

My passion for computer science started freshman year in high school. That's when I got into programming and began to teach myself web development through free online courses and tools. I was entranced. I could dive into projects and allow myself to get into a zone. Hours would fly by as I developed machine learning algorithms and researched new theories. I can spend days trying to solve a complex problem. When I can’t find a solution, I push through because I want that instant rush of joy in solving something. It’s indescribable. I knew this was something I wanted to pursue as a career. Since then, I have been learning other coding languages such as SQL, Java, and Swift. Coding has become my comfort zone. I realized this when I launched my first app (CardCart) through Apple, when over 700 people downloaded my app I understood that I had impacted lives. With this power I’ve learned to create software that makes life easier for people, and that concept is why I believe computer science is such a useful, and practical skill to have. I’m inspired by how broad a reach technology has. Even parents like mine, weak in English, are able incorporate programs like mine into their business to enhance efficiency. I want to become an innovator; people are waiting to be helped. Privileged people may have additional access to tools and technology. However, my goal is to provide products to those who don’t have access. I want to use my skills and passion in computer science to make the world more efficient, and I want to be able to create products that people like my parents can use.
